## Design Context

### Users
Friends of the creator, primarily using on mobile phones. The typical moment of use: standing somewhere, hungry, reaching for the phone to get a quick recommendation. The context is casual, personal, and social — this isn't a productivity tool, it's a comfort tool.

### Brand Personality
**3 words: handmade, healing, local**

The app should feel like a warm notebook a friend keeps of their favorite spots — not a polished tech product. It evokes the feeling of being taken care of through food (療癒). Texture and life-like imperfection are welcome: subtle hand-drawn food illustration motifs in the background, warm off-white surfaces, and playful iconography (footprints for walking distance, a bus icon for transit) that make the interface feel alive and personal rather than generic.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Light mode — warm, off-white surfaces (not pure white), like aged paper or a well-used recipe card
- **Color palette**: Warm neutrals tinted toward amber/orange. The primary accent is a warm orange (reference: `#E46C0A` already in use) — reserved for the main CTA. Avoid cool grays; everything should lean warm.
- **Anti-references**: No tech-y feel — no blue system colors, no sharp geometric grids, no dashboard-style layouts. Should not look like a SaaS product or a food delivery app (Uber Eats, Foodpanda).
- **Texture**: Subtle background pattern with faint hand-drawn food ingredient illustrations (linework, not filled shapes). Low contrast against the surface — atmosphere, not decoration.
- **Iconography**: Contextual and playful — footprint icon for walking, bus for transit, etc. Icons should feel expressive, not purely functional.

### Design Principles
1. **Comfort over efficiency** — The app is opened when hungry and seeking reassurance. Every interaction should feel warm and unhurried, never clinical.
2. **Texture over polish** — Imperfect, handmade visual qualities (subtle patterns, organic shapes, expressive icons) create the lived-in feel that makes this feel personal, not corporate.
3. **Warmth is a palette rule** — Every color must lean warm. No pure blacks, no cool grays, no achromatic neutrals. Tint everything toward amber.
4. **Playfulness through detail** — Small touches (distance icons, the phrasing of buttons, micro-animations) carry the personality. Don't rely on big visual statements; let the details do the work.
5. **Mobile-first, thumb-friendly** — Primary actions must be easily reachable one-handed. Large tap targets, generous spacing, no tiny controls.

## Owner Preferences (Constraints)

These are the project owner's personal rules — confirmed through feedback in design conversations. **Treat them as hard constraints**: they override impeccable's defaults when in conflict, and override anything you might infer from training data. Each entry is anchored to the specific moment it was decided so future-you can judge whether it still applies.

### Likes (do these — confirmed by acceptance)

- **Brand orange `#E46C0A` as the only primary CTA colour.** Implemented as `oklch(0.625 0.175 45)`. Every "main action" surface must reach for this one hue. Confirmed 2026-05-03 in `design.md`.
- **jf-openhuninn 2.1 (粉圓) is the typeface.** Single face, single weight; do not pair with a second display font. Confirmed 2026-05-03 when explicitly chosen over the impeccable default font selection flow.
- **Tiramisu is the brand mark.** Layered cocoa-cream-coffee block with a small face. Replaced an earlier onigiri design at the owner's request, 2026-05-03. Do not switch back without owner approval.
- **日系療癒手帳風 (Japanese-zakka journal feel)** is the committed aesthetic. Hand-drawn texture, organic shapes, paper-like surfaces. Source: `docs/impeccable/ref/design.md`.
- **Pudding-bounce on press only** is the signature micro-interaction (`@keyframes pudding-bounce`, ease-pudding curve). Confirmed by acceptance after first pass; do not extend it to hover or entrance animations.
- **Hamburger menu is mobile-only.** Visible at `<sm` (640px); ≥sm shows inline nav. Confirmed 2026-05-03 — owner explicitly rejected always-visible hamburger because brand link and menu items got visually conflated.
- **Logo must be visually distinct from menu items.** Brand mark (icon + wordmark) sits on the left, nav links on the right, with clear hierarchy. Same feedback as above.
- **Reading veil over patterned backgrounds.** The `.zakka-content` wash behind content was added 2026-05-03 after owner reported the bg pattern interfering with readability. Apply on every page wrapper that has body text on top of `zakka-paper`.

### Dislikes (avoid these — explicit rejects)

- **No tech-y / SaaS / dashboard look.** No blue system colours, no sharp geometric grids, no "stat card" templates. Source: `design.md` Q5 ("過於科技感"). Anti-references the owner named: Uber Eats, Foodpanda.
- **No cool greys, no achromatic neutrals.** Every neutral must lean warm (amber-tinted). No pure `#000` or `#fff`. Source: `design.md` and Design Principle #3.
- **No serif fonts on this app.** The earlier Noto Serif TC default was explicitly rejected; reverting breaks the system.
- **No background pattern denser/heavier than the current tuning.** Owner flagged the bg motifs as competing with text on 2026-05-03. Hard caps: `opacity ≤ 0.20` on desktop, `≤ 0.15` on `<640px`. Increase tile size before raising opacity.
- **No `text-muted-foreground` on the patterned bg for important text.** Use `text-foreground/75` minimum. Owner flagged NavBar contrast specifically — applies wherever else this token would have been used over `zakka-paper`.
- **No always-on hamburger menu.** Only at narrow breakpoints. (See Likes.)
- **No bouncing on hover, no elastic easing on entrance.** Pudding-bounce is press-only. (See Likes.)
- **No icon library on brand surfaces.** Hand-drawn SVG only for hero / brand mark / decorative icons. lucide / heroicons may be used for purely utility contexts (close, chevron) if needed.

### Soft Preferences / Open Questions

These are weaker — still useful to honour, but ask before letting them block something better:

- The owner has not committed to specific tags' colour mapping yet. `--matcha` and `--azuki` exist as placeholders for "vegetable/health" and "dessert/comfort" categories respectively, but specific tag → colour mapping is still TBD.
- Empty / loading / error states for `recommend` and `restaurants` pages have not been designed. When implementing them, ask before inventing tone for these — the owner may have specific copy ideas.
- Whether to optimise the 4.9MB TTF + 2.1MB PNG bundle. Both have been flagged as "production concern, not blocker"; defer until owner asks.

### How to Extend This Section

When you receive feedback that locks in a new preference (positive or negative), add an entry here. Structure each entry as:

> **The rule itself, in one line.**  Brief context — the originating feedback or design moment, and the date. Add to Likes if the owner explicitly accepted; Dislikes if they explicitly rejected; Soft if it's inferred from a single offhand comment.

If a constraint conflicts with a newer one, leave the old entry but mark it `~~struck-through~~` with a note pointing to the superseding rule. Do not silently delete history — the owner may want to revisit.

### Paper Background

Three stacked layers form the surface; never replace one without touching the others.

1. **`body` color** — `--background`, the warm linen tint
2. **`.zakka-paper::before`** — `/zakka-background.png` tile (hand-drawn food motifs)
   - `background-size: 340px` (desktop), `380px` (≤640px)
   - `opacity: 0.18` (desktop), `0.15` (≤640px)
   - `mix-blend-mode: multiply` so the warm tint shows through
   - **Do not raise opacity past 0.20** — body text loses contrast
3. **`.zakka-paper::after`** — programmatic radial-gradient grain at 0.03–0.04 opacity, adds tooth without competing with the food motifs

**Reading veil — `.zakka-content`:** wraps the main content column with a soft warm wash (`oklch(0.97 0.013 76 / 0.78)`, rounded 24/28px, inset -8/-16px on mobile, -12/-32px ≥720px). Without it, body text fights the pattern. Apply to top-level page wrappers.

### Typography

**Single typeface, single weight: jf-openhuninn 2.1** (粉圓). Loaded via `next/font/local` from `public/fonts/jf-openhuninn-2.1.ttf`, exposed as `--font-zakka`. Drives both `--font-sans` and `--font-heading`.

- The font's character carries the brand — don't pair it with a second display face
- 4.9MB raw TTF; subset to 繁中 + ASCII + woff2 before production (`pyftsubset` + `fonttools`)
- For new TS/TSX writing fonts: never reach for Noto Serif TC, Noto Sans TC, Inter, or any font in impeccable's reflex_fonts_to_reject list

**Hierarchy:**
- Hero title: `text-[2.6rem] leading-[1.05] tracking-tight` (no font-weight — jf-openhuninn ships single weight)
- Section heading: `text-sm` with bookmark/dot icon prefix
- Body: base size, `text-foreground` for primary, `text-foreground/75` for secondary on patterned bg
- Microcopy / metadata: `text-xs tracking-[0.08em]` (slight tracking for stamp feel)
- Date stamps and similar journal accents: short tracked text framed by hairline rules

### Iconography & Brand Mark

**Brand mark: tiramisu.** Layered cocoa-cream-coffee block. Replaces the earlier onigiri mark.

- **Hero variant** ([app/page.tsx](app/page.tsx) `<Tiramisu />`): 128×128 viewBox, tilted -2.5° for hand-drawn feel, includes face (eyes + cheeks + smile), coffee-bean garnish, cocoa dust dots, three brand-orange sparkles in twinkle animation
- **Mini variant** ([components/NavBar.tsx](components/NavBar.tsx) `<BrandMark />`): 24×24 viewBox in a 32×32 brand-tinted rounded square (`bg-brand/12 ring-brand/25`). Strips face + coffee bean — at this size the layered stripes carry the identity alone. Three cocoa-dust dots remain.

**Icon style rules:**
- All icons are inline SVG, hand-drawn feel: `strokeLinecap="round"`, `strokeLinejoin="round"`, varied stroke weights
- Never import an icon library that ships uniform geometric icons (lucide-react/feather/heroicons) for brand surfaces — they break the handmade tone
- Functional UI icons (search, close, chevron) may use simple stroke shapes but should still favor brushy lines over crisp geometry
- Icons in CTAs use `currentColor` so they inherit button text color

### Motion

Three named easings live in `:root`:

| Token | Curve | Use |
|---|---|---|
| `--ease-out-quart` | `cubic-bezier(0.25, 1, 0.5, 1)` | Default deceleration for state changes |
| `--ease-pudding` | `cubic-bezier(0.34, 1.56, 0.64, 1)` | Press-only overshoot (the "布丁" feel) |

**Named animations:**
- `@keyframes pudding-bounce` (360ms) — squash-and-stretch on `:active` only. Apply via `.pudding-press` class. Never on hover or entrance — this is the user's "按下後像布丁" brief, not a default animation flavor
- `@keyframes sparkle-twinkle` (2.4s, infinite) — opacity + scale pulse for SVG sparkle accents. Stagger `animationDelay` per element

**Hard rules:**
- Animate `transform` / `opacity` only — never width/height/padding/margin
- Use `grid-template-rows: 0fr → 1fr` for collapsing/expanding height (used by the hamburger panel)
- All motion ≤400ms unless explicitly justified (sparkles loop, so longer is fine)
- Every animation must respect `@media (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce)` — turn it off, don't slow it down

### Layout & Components

**Page shell** ([app/layout.tsx](app/layout.tsx)): `max-w-2xl mx-auto px-4 py-8` content column, `<body class="zakka-paper">`, sticky `<NavBar />` with `z-30 backdrop-blur-sm`. New pages should opt into `.zakka-content` on their root wrapper for the reading veil.

**Navigation pattern** ([components/NavBar.tsx](components/NavBar.tsx)):
- Brand mark + wordmark on the left, always visible
- `≥sm` (640px+): inline nav links on the right (primary action as filled brand pill, others as ghost text-links with brand-color underline when active)
- `<sm`: hamburger button on the right + slide-down panel below the header
- The slide-down panel uses `grid-template-rows` for animation, `tabIndex={-1}` on hidden links, closes on Esc / route change / click-outside
- Hamburger icon is three brush-stroke `<span>` bars that morph to X on open — not an icon-font glyph

**Primary CTA pattern** (see hero on [app/page.tsx](app/page.tsx)):
- Rounded-full pill, `px-6 py-4 text-lg`
- Stacked shadow for "press depth": `shadow-[0_6px_0_-1px_oklch(0.40_0.140_45)]` resting → 5px on hover (lift) → 2px on `:active` (sink) — combined with `active:translate-y-1`
- `.pudding-press` class for the squish-on-press animation
- Always paired with a ghost outline secondary action below it

**Secondary action pattern:**
- `rounded-full border border-border bg-card/60` — softer than primary, distinct from disabled
- Smaller padding, base font size

**Card surfaces:**
- `bg-card/90` minimum opacity over the patterned bg (lower → text becomes hard to read)
- `border-border/80` (less than full opacity to keep edges soft)
- Use **dashed** borders for internal dividers inside a card — matches the journal/手帳 feel

**List items in cards:**
- Use small brand-color dots (`h-1 w-1 rounded-full bg-brand/60`) as bullets, not `list-disc`
- Toggle indicators: a `›` character that rotates 90° on `group-open` — feels handwritten

### Anti-patterns Specific to This Project

In addition to impeccable's general absolute_bans (no left-border stripes, no gradient text), this project also avoids:

- **No serif fonts.** The brand voice is rounded-friendly. Reverting to Noto Serif TC (the historical default) breaks the entire system
- **No icon-library imports for brand surfaces.** Hand-drawn SVG only for hero, brand mark, and decorative icons. Lucide / heroicons OK for utility-only contexts (close, chevron) if you must
- **No fully-saturated `bg-card`.** Always `/90` or lighter so the bg pattern faintly shows through — that translucency is the "paper inset" effect
- **No `text-muted-foreground` on the patterned bg for important text.** Use `text-foreground/75` minimum
- **No bouncing on hover.** The pudding bounce is for press only — hover is for cursor-equipped users who'd find a bounce annoying on every mouse-over
